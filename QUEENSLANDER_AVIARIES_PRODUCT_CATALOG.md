### Parasite Control
- **Mite & Lice Spray** - External parasite treatment
- **Worming Medication** - Internal parasite control
- **Cage Disinfectant** - Deep-clean parasite elimination
- **Air Sac Mite Treatment** - Respiratory parasite care

### Specific Conditions
- **Feather Plucking Deterrents** - Behavioral/health sprays
- **Anti-Fungal Treatments** - Crop/skin infections
- **Respiratory Support** - Breathing aid supplements
- **Joint Support** - Arthritis care for older birds
- **Molting Support** - Extra nutrition during feather replacement

### Specialty Veterinary
- **Prescription Medications** - Require vet authorization
- **Antibiotics** - Bacterial infections (vet prescribed)
- **Anti-Inflammatory** - Pain/swelling management
- **Nebulizer Medications** - Respiratory treatments

### Health Product Guidelines
- ⚠️ **ALWAYS recommend vet visit for sick birds**
- Many products require proper dosing by weight
- Keep medicine away from heat/moisture
- Check expiration dates frequently
- Never sell prescription items without vet authorization
- Document all medicine sales (customer name, date, product)

### Safe Foods Reference (Not medicine, but health-related)
**SAFE for Birds:**
- Apples, bananas, berries, melons
- Carrots, broccoli, leafy greens, sweet potato
- Cooked rice, pasta, beans
- Unsalted nuts (almonds, walnuts)

**TOXIC - Never Sell/Recommend:**
- Avocado, chocolate, caffeine, salt
- Onions, garlic, alcohol
- Apple seeds, fruit pits
- Teflon fumes (not food, but important safety note)

## STOCK ORGANIZATION TIPS

### High-Turnover Items (Check Daily)
- Popular seed blends (Budgie, Cockatiel)
- Nutriblend pellets (all sizes)
- Millet sprays
- Cuttlebone
- Small/medium toys
- Stainless steel bowls

### Seasonal Fluctuations
- **Spring/Summer:** Breeding supplies up, nest boxes, calcium
- **Fall/Winter:** Heat lamps, warming perches, vitamin D
- **Christmas:** Seasonal toys, gift sets
- **Back to School:** Travel cages (families getting new pets)

### Shelf Life Awareness
- **Seed:** 6-12 months (watch for bugs/mold)
- **Pellets:** 12-18 months sealed
- **Treats:** Check individual packaging
- **Medicine:** Strictly follow expiration dates
- **Toys:** No expiration, but check for damage

### Customer Questions - Be Ready
1. "What's the difference between seed and pellets?" 
   - Pellets = complete nutrition, seed = more natural but needs supplementing
2. "How many toys does my bird need?"
   - At least 3-4, rotate weekly for mental stimulation
3. "What size cage for my [species]?"
   - Wingspan x 2 minimum width, tall enough to fully extend tail
4. "My bird is plucking feathers, what product helps?"
   - Recommend vet visit first, then discuss toys/diet/supplements
5. "Can I just feed seeds?"
   - Birds need variety: pellets, fresh foods, treats for balanced diet
