## ðŸ—£ï¸ Verbal Communication Skills

### Effective Speaking
**Daily Huddles:**
- **Be brief** - 5 minutes max
- **Be clear** - Simple words, short sentences
- **Be loud** - Project your voice
- **Make eye contact** - Engage your team

**One-on-One Conversations:**
- **Listen first** - Let them talk 70%, you talk 30%
- **Ask open questions** - "What happened?" not "Did you do X?"
- **Confirm understanding** - "So what you're saying is..."
- **Be specific** - Not "do better," but "I need 60 items/hour"

**Difficult Conversations:**
- **Private location** - Never criticize publicly
- **State facts** - "I observed X" not "You always Y"
- **Focus on behavior** - Not personality
- **Give chance to respond** - Hear their side
- **End with clear expectation** - "Going forward, I need..."

---

### Communication Frameworks

**SBAR (Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation)**
Use when reporting to your manager:
- **S**ituation: "We're behind on today's orders"
- **B**ackground: "Two people called out and we got extra orders"
- **A**ssessment: "We'll miss target by 20 orders"
- **R**ecommendation: "Can we get help from store or approve overtime?"

**GROW (Goal, Reality, Options, Way Forward)**
Use for coaching conversations:
- **G**oal: "What are you trying to achieve?"
- **R**eality: "What's the current situation?"
- **O**ptions: "What could you try?"
- **W**ay Forward: "What will you do?"

**DESC (Describe, Express, Specify, Consequences)**
Use for addressing problems:
- **D**escribe: "You've been 15 minutes late 3 times this week"
- **E**xpress: "This creates problems because..."
- **S**pecify: "I need you here on time at 8:00 AM"
- **C**onsequences: "If this continues, we'll need to..."

## ðŸ§  Mental Frameworks & Mindsets

### Decision-Making Models

**Eisenhower Matrix (Urgent/Important)**
```
        Urgent          | Not Urgent
Important: DO NOW      | SCHEDULE
Not Important: DELEGATE| ELIMINATE
```

**5 Whys (Root Cause Analysis)**
Ask "why" 5 times to find real problem:
- Order was late â†’ Why? Picker was slow
- Why slow? Couldn't find items
- Why couldn't find? Wrong location in system
- Why wrong location? Data entry error on receiving
- Why data entry error? No training on new system
**Real problem:** Need better receiving training

**Cost/Benefit Analysis**
For any decision:
- What does it cost? (time, money, effort)
- What's the benefit? (productivity, morale, safety)
- Worth it? If benefit > cost, probably yes

### Time Management Techniques

**Pomodoro Technique**
- Work focused for 25 minutes
- Take 5-minute break
- Repeat 4 times
- Take longer break (15-30 minutes)
**Good for:** Focused work like scheduling, reports

**Time Blocking**
- Assign specific times for specific tasks
- Example: 8-9 AM = Huddles, 9-11 AM = Floor management, 11-12 PM = Admin
**Good for:** Preventing reactive management

**Eat the Frog**
- Do hardest/most important task first
- Everything else feels easier after
**Good for:** Tackling difficult conversations, complex problems

**2-Minute Rule**
- If task takes <2 minutes, do it now
- Don't add to to-do list
**Good for:** Preventing task pile-up

---

### Leadership Mindsets

**Growth Mindset vs. Fixed Mindset**
- Fixed: "They're just not good at it"
- Growth: "They haven't learned it YET"
**Application:** Believe your team can improve with training

**Servant Leadership**
- Your job: Remove obstacles for your team
- Not: Tell them what to do and leave
**Application:** Ask "What do you need from me?" regularly

**Trust but Verify**
- Give autonomy (trust)
- Check results (verify)
- Not micromanaging, not blind faith
**Application:** "You handle this. I'll check back at end of day."

**Lead by Example**
- Want team on time? You arrive early
- Want hard work? Work hard yourself
- Want respect? Show respect
**Remember:** Team mirrors your behavior

---

### Stress Management

**Quick Stress Relief (1-5 minutes)**
- Deep breathing: 4 seconds in, 7 seconds hold, 8 seconds out
- Walk around building
- Cold water on face
- Stretch at desk
- Call a friend

**Long-Term Stress Prevention**
- Regular exercise (even 20 minutes)
- Adequate sleep (7-8 hours)
- Healthy eating (skip the energy drinks)
- Hobby outside work
- Set boundaries (work stays at work)

**Signs You're Burning Out**
- Constant exhaustion
- Cynical/negative about everything
- Decreased performance
- Getting sick often
- Not enjoying anything
**Action:** Talk to your manager, take time off, reassess priorities

---

### Conflict Resolution Approaches

**Collaborative (Win-Win)**
- Best when: Both sides have valid points
- Approach: "How can we both get what we need?"

**Compromising (Meet in Middle)**
- Best when: Quick solution needed, both give a little
- Approach: "What can each of us give up?"

**Accommodating (Let Them Win)**
- Best when: Not important to you, relationship matters more
- Approach: "Sure, we'll do it your way"

**Competing (I Win)**
- Best when: Safety issue, clear policy, emergency
- Approach: "This is how it must be done"

**Avoiding (Walk Away)**
- Best when: Cooling-off period needed, trivial issue
- Approach: "Let's talk about this tomorrow"

## ðŸ’¡ Pro Tips

### Technology
- **Automate repetitive tasks** - If you do it daily, find a tool
- **Use templates** - Don't recreate from scratch
- **Back up everything** - Cloud storage is cheap
- **Learn keyboard shortcuts** - Save hours per week
- **Use two monitors** - Productivity boost

### Physical Tools
- **Label everything** - Locations, equipment, supplies
- **Keep things where you use them** - Don't walk far for common tools
- **Maintain equipment** - Broken tools cost more than new ones
- **Organize your workspace** - Clean desk = clear mind

### Communication
- **Over-communicate** - When in doubt, communicate more
- **Document conversations** - Especially important ones
- **Use the right channel** - Text for quick, email for formal, face-to-face for sensitive
- **Follow up in writing** - After verbal agreements

### Mental Frameworks
- **Start with why** - Help team understand purpose
- **Think systems** - Fix processes, not just problems
- **Embrace feedback** - Criticism helps you grow
- **Reflect daily** - What worked? What didn't?
