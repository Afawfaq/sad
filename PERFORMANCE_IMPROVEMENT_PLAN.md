## IMPORTANT NOTES

### For Supervisors

**Documentation Best Practices:**
- Be specific, objective, and factual
- Use measurable data whenever possible
- Avoid subjective language or personal opinions
- Focus on behaviors and outcomes, not personality
- Keep detailed records of all meetings and observations
- Be consistent and fair in expectations

**During PIP Period:**
- Provide regular, constructive feedback
- Document everything (good and bad)
- Offer support and encouragement
- Be available for questions and guidance
- Adjust plan if circumstances change
- Maintain confidentiality

**Legal Considerations:**
- Consult HR before finalizing PIP
- Ensure compliance with company policies
- Document thoroughly to protect against claims
- Treat all employees consistently
- Be prepared to defend decisions

---

### For Employees

**Your Rights:**
- Ask questions about expectations
- Request specific examples
- Seek clarification on goals
- Request additional support/resources
- Provide your perspective on the situation
- Have representation (if company policy allows)

**Your Responsibilities:**
- Take the PIP seriously
- Actively work on improvement
- Communicate proactively with supervisor
- Ask for help when needed
- Track your own progress
- Be honest about challenges

**Success Tips:**
- Focus on one goal at a time
- Break large goals into small steps
- Celebrate small wins
- Seek feedback regularly
- Use available resources
- Maintain a positive attitude
- Don't be afraid to ask questions
