# EDGE CASE SCENARIOS GUIDE

Unusual situations and how to handle them when standard procedures don't apply.

## üî• Emergency Situations

### Fire or Evacuation
**Action:**
1. Sound alarm if not already sounding
2. Evacuate all staff immediately via designated exits
3. Account for all team members at assembly point
4. Call 911 if not already called
5. DO NOT re-enter building
6. Contact upper management
7. Document incident after everyone is safe

**Who to notify:** Emergency services, upper management, HR

---

### Severe Weather (Tornado, Hurricane, Earthquake)
**Action:**
1. Move team to designated safe area
2. Stay away from windows
3. Monitor weather alerts
4. Do not leave until all-clear given
5. Check for injuries
6. Assess building damage before resuming operations

**Decision:** Safety > Operations, always

---

### Power Outage
**Short term (< 1 hour):**
- Continue work that doesn't require power
- Use emergency lighting
- Prioritize time-sensitive orders manually

**Long term (> 1 hour):**
- Send non-essential staff home with manager approval
- Secure inventory and facility
- Contact utility company for ETA
- Update customers on delays

---

### Medical Emergency
**Action:**
1. Call 911 immediately
2. Administer first aid if trained
3. Do NOT move injured person unless immediate danger
4. Clear area for paramedics
5. Assign someone to meet ambulance
6. Document incident
7. Notify HR and management
8. Preserve scene if workplace injury

**Remember:** You're not a doctor - get professional help

---

## üí∞ Financial/Security Edge Cases

### Suspected Theft by Employee
**DO NOT confront directly**

**Action:**
1. Document what you observed (date, time, what you saw)
2. Review security footage if available
3. Report to HR and upper management immediately
4. Let them investigate - this is above your pay grade
5. Do NOT discuss with other employees
6. Continue normal supervision until directed otherwise

**Legal note:** False accusations can result in lawsuits

---

### Suspected Theft by Customer
**Action:**
1. Do NOT physically stop or detain
2. Note description, time, items
3. Review security footage
4. Contact security/police per company policy
5. Document incident
6. Let authorities handle it

**Never:** Chase, physically confront, or accuse without proof

---

### Finding Large Amount of Cash/Valuable Item
**Action:**
1. Don't touch it (fingerprints)
2. Call manager and security immediately
3. Secure area
4. Document where found, when, by whom
5. Have witness present
6. Turn over to management with witness signature

**Protect yourself:** Never handle alone

---

### Employee Shows Up Intoxicated/High
**Action:**
1. Pull them aside privately
2. "I'm concerned about your safety. You don't seem well. Let me call someone to pick you up."
3. Do NOT let them work
4. Do NOT let them drive
5. Call manager/HR immediately
6. Arrange safe transportation
7. Document incident
8. Follow company policy on discipline

**Safety first:** Impaired employees are dangerous to themselves and others

---

## üë• People Edge Cases

### Employee Threatens Violence
**Immediate threat:**
1. Call 911
2. Evacuate area if safe to do so
3. Do NOT confront
4. Secure yourself and others

**Verbal threat only:**
1. Take it seriously
2. Remove employee from floor
3. Contact HR and security immediately
4. Do NOT send them home (they may return)
5. Document exact words used
6. Get witness statements

**Never ignore threats**

---

### Employee Has Emotional Breakdown at Work
**Action:**
1. Move to private area
2. "I can see you're upset. Do you want to talk or would you like some time?"
3. Listen if they want to talk
4. Offer: "Would you like to go home? Call someone?"
5. Contact HR if serious (suicide mention, severe distress)
6. Follow up next day with wellness check

**You're not a therapist:** Be kind but know your limits

---

### Employee Reports Harassment/Discrimination
**Action:**
1. Listen without judgment
2. "Thank you for telling me. This is serious. I'm going to report this to HR right away."
3. Take notes on what they said
4. Report to HR immediately - same day
5. Do NOT investigate yourself
6. Do NOT confront alleged harasser
7. Protect employee from retaliation

**Legal requirement:** You MUST report, or YOU can be liable

---

### Two Employees Dating (Or Breaking Up)
**No problem if:**
- Not supervisor/subordinate relationship
- No workplace disruption
- Professional at work

**Problem if:**
- Drama affecting work
- Others complaining
- Supervisor dating direct report (conflict of interest)

**Action:**
- Talk to both: "Personal life is personal, but work is work. Keep it professional here."
- If serious issues: Involve HR
- If supervisor/subordinate: HR must know (potential legal issue)

---

### Employee's Family Member Dies
**Immediate:**
- "I'm so sorry for your loss. Take the time you need."
- Offer to call someone for them
- Send them home

**Follow-up:**
- Check bereavement leave policy
- Coordinate coverage
- Welcome them back gently
- Be patient with performance for a while

**Don't ask:** Details about the death

---

## üì¶ Operations Edge Cases

### System Completely Down, Can't Ship
**Action:**
1. Contact IT immediately with ticket
2. Document orders that can't ship
3. Contact customers via phone/email
4. Prepare manual workarounds if possible
5. Keep team productive on other tasks
6. Update management on impact

**Communication:** Under-promise, over-deliver on timeline

---

### Receive Wrong Shipment (Not Your Order)
**Action:**
1. Don't unpack or use items
2. Check packing slip vs. label
3. Contact vendor immediately
4. Take photos as proof
5. Arrange return pickup
6. Track your actual order
7. Document for receiving records

**Don't:** Just keep it and say nothing

---

### Discover Product Recall/Safety Issue
**Action:**
1. STOP shipping that product immediately
2. Quarantine all units
3. Contact management immediately
4. Check if already shipped (need to contact customers)
5. Document lot numbers
6. Follow company recall procedures

**Liability:** Shipping recalled products can get company sued

---

### Vendor Delivers to Wrong Address (Your Stuff Elsewhere)
**Action:**
1. Track down where it went
2. Contact vendor AND receiving location
3. Arrange retrieval or re-delivery
4. Document for vendor charge-back
5. Expedite replacement order if critical
6. Learn: Update receiving procedures

---

### Customer Paid But Order Can't Be Fulfilled (Out of Stock, Discontinued)
**Action:**
1. Contact customer immediately (phone call, not email)
2. Options: Substitute item, backorder with date, full refund
3. Let customer choose
4. Process refund same day if requested
5. Document for accounting
6. Update inventory system

**Make it right:** Customer experience matters

---

## üåê Technology Edge Cases

### Credit Card System Down
**Options:**
1. Manual imprinter (if you have one)
2. Record card info to process later (secure method only)
3. Accept alternative payment (Venmo, cash, check per policy)
4. Let them pick up/pay later if trusted customer

**Never:** Write down full card numbers on paper

---

### Can't Access Required System (Password Reset, Locked Out)
**Immediate:**
1. Contact IT help desk
2. Use backup system if available
3. Identify critical vs. can-wait tasks
4. Work offline if possible
5. Communicate delays to customers

**Prevention:** Have IT contacts saved in multiple places

---

### Email/Communication System Down
**Workarounds:**
1. Use personal cell phone (save numbers in advance)
2. Text messages
3. In-person communication
4. Write notes for documentation
5. Update when system returns

**Lesson:** Don't rely on one communication channel

---

## üè¢ Facility Edge Cases

### Plumbing Failure (Floods, No Water)
**Flooding:**
- Evacuate affected area
- Turn off water if you can
- Call maintenance/plumber
- Move inventory to dry areas
- Document damage for insurance

**No water:**
- Can staff work without bathrooms? (Probably not)
- Arrange porta-potty or send home
- Contact maintenance for ETA
- Safety issue if no running water for handwashing

---

### Heating/AC Failure
**Extreme heat:**
- Provide water frequently
- Take more breaks
- Watch for heat exhaustion signs
- Send home if unsafe (>95¬∞F)

**Extreme cold:**
- Extra layers permitted
- Heated break area
- Shorter shifts if unbearable
- Safety issue if below freezing

**Decision criteria:** If you wouldn't want to work in it, probably shouldn't ask them to

---

### Pest Infestation Discovered
**Action:**
1. Document with photos
2. Quarantine affected area/inventory
3. Contact pest control immediately
4. Notify management and health dept if required
5. May need to cease operations in affected area
6. Deep clean after treatment

**Don't hide it:** Health code violations can shut you down

---

## üìã Documentation Edge Cases

### Employee Claims They Didn't Get Written Warning They Signed
**Protection:**
- Always make copies
- Have them initial all pages
- Email copy to them same day
- Keep in file and give copy to HR
- Use date stamp

**If they claim forgery:** HR and legal handle it, not you

---

### You Made a Serious Mistake (Shipped Wrong Order, Broke Equipment)
**Action:**
1. Own it immediately - don't hide it
2. Inform your manager right away
3. "I made a mistake. Here's what happened, here's how I'm fixing it."
4. Document what went wrong
5. Implement prevention for next time
6. Learn from it

**Mistakes happen:** Honesty is the best policy

---

### Asked to Do Something You Think is Wrong/Illegal
**Action:**
1. Ask for clarification: "Can you explain why we're doing it this way?"
2. Express concern: "I'm worried this might be [reason]"
3. Request written instruction
4. If still concerned: Document conversation
5. If clearly illegal: Talk to HR, legal, or don't do it
6. Protect yourself: "I need this in writing"

**Your career:** Don't do illegal things, even if told to

---

## ü§ù Customer Edge Cases

### Extremely Angry Customer (Screaming, Threatening)
**Action:**
1. Stay calm - don't match their energy
2. "I understand you're upset. Let's solve this."
3. Listen without interrupting
4. If threatening: "I want to help, but I can't if you're threatening me."
5. If continues: "I'm going to need to end this call/ask you to leave"
6. Call security/police if necessary
7. Document incident

**Never:** Argue back or be rude

---

### Customer Wants to Return Item Bought Months Ago
**Policy first, exception second:**
1. "Our policy is [X days]"
2. If outside policy: "Let me see what I can do"
3. Check reason: Defective? Changed mind?
4. Use judgment: Good customer? Small amount?
5. If you approve exception: Note it
6. If deny: "I understand you're disappointed, but our policy is firm on this"

**Balance:** Customer satisfaction vs. policy

---

### Customer Wants Discount You Can't Authorize
**Action:**
1. "I want to help. Let me check with my manager."
2. Present case to manager with recommendation
3. Return with answer
4. If denied: "I tried, but this is the best we can do: [alternative]"

**Never:** Promise something you can't deliver

---

## üí° General Edge Case Principles

### When You Don't Know What to Do
1. **Escalate:** "This is outside my experience. I need to involve [manager/HR/legal]"
2. **Don't guess:** Better to admit you don't know
3. **Document:** Write down what happened
4. **Ask for help:** No one expects you to know everything
5. **Learn:** After resolution, understand what should have been done

### CYA (Cover Your Assets)
- **Document everything unusual**
- **Get things in writing**
- **Have witnesses for serious matters**
- **Follow company policy**
- **When in doubt, escalate**

### Priority in Edge Cases
1. **Safety** - Human safety always first
2. **Legal compliance** - Don't break laws
3. **Company policy** - Follow procedures
4. **Business needs** - Keep operations running
5. **Everything else** - Can be figured out later

**Remember:** Edge cases are called that because they're rare. Don't stress about knowing every possible scenario. Use good judgment, prioritize safety and ethics, and escalate when needed.
