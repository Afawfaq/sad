VERBAL COMMUNICATION SKILLS

Effective Speaking:
Daily Huddles: Be brief (5 min), be clear, be loud, make eye contact
One-on-One: Listen 70%, talk 30%, ask open questions, be specific
Difficult Conversations: Private location, state facts, focus on behavior, end with clear expectation

Communication Frameworks:

SBAR (Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation)
Use when reporting to manager

GROW (Goal, Reality, Options, Way Forward)
Use for coaching conversations

DESC (Describe, Express, Specify, Consequences)
Use for addressing problems

Multilingual Communication:
- Speak slowly not loudly
- Use simple words
- Avoid idioms
- Use pictures/demonstrations
- Write important information
- Use translation apps
- Learn key phrases
- Pair with bilingual buddy

Body Language:
Thumbs up = Good, Pointing = Direction, Hand out = Stop, Waving = Come here, Head nod = Yes, Shaking head = No

MENTAL FRAMEWORKS & MINDSETS

Decision-Making Models:

Eisenhower Matrix (Urgent/Important)
Important + Urgent: DO NOW
Important + Not Urgent: SCHEDULE
Not Important + Urgent: DELEGATE
Not Important + Not Urgent: ELIMINATE

5 Whys (Root Cause Analysis)
Ask "why" 5 times to find real problem

Cost/Benefit Analysis
What does it cost? What's the benefit? Worth it?

Time Management Techniques:

Pomodoro: 25 min work, 5 min break, repeat
Time Blocking: Assign specific times for specific tasks
Eat the Frog: Do hardest task first
2-Minute Rule: If < 2 minutes, do it now

Leadership Mindsets:

Growth Mindset: They can learn and improve
Servant Leadership: Remove obstacles for team
Trust but Verify: Give autonomy, check results
Lead by Example: Team mirrors your behavior

Stress Management:

Quick Relief (1-5 min): Deep breathing, walk, cold water, stretch
Long-Term: Exercise, sleep, healthy eating, hobbies, boundaries
Burnout Signs: Exhaustion, cynical, decreased performance, getting sick, not enjoying anything

Conflict Resolution:

Collaborative (Win-Win): "How can we both get what we need?"
Compromising (Meet in Middle): "What can each of us give up?"
Accommodating (Let Them Win): "Sure, your way"
Competing (I Win): "This is how it must be"
Avoiding (Walk Away): "Let's talk tomorrow"

SKILL DEVELOPMENT PLAN

Month 1-2: Foundation
- Master templates
- Learn WMS inside out
- Build team relationships
- Observe other managers
- Get feedback

Month 3-4: Communication
- Practice SBAR reporting
- Run huddles confidently
- Have difficult conversation
- Read management book
- Join industry group

Month 5-6: Leadership
- Mentor new team member
- Implement process improvement
- Track and improve KPIs
- Take online course
- Teach team something

Ongoing:
- Read 1 book per quarter
- Attend 1 training per month
- Get team feedback regularly
- Set development goals
- Document learnings

PRO TIPS

Technology:

- Use templates

Physical Tools:

Communication:

- Use right channel
- Follow up in writing

Mental Frameworks:
- Start with why
- Think systems
- Embrace feedback
- Reflect daily
