# Verbal Communication Skills

Effective communication techniques for warehouse supervisors.

### Daily Huddles
- **Be brief** - 5 minutes max
- **Be clear** - Simple words, short sentences
- **Be loud** - Project your voice
- **Make eye contact** - Engage your team

### One-on-One Conversations
- **Listen first** - Let them talk 70%, you talk 30%
- **Ask open questions** - "What happened?" not "Did you do X?"
- **Confirm understanding** - "So what you're saying is..."
- **Be specific** - Not "do better," but "I need 60 items/hour"

### Difficult Conversations
- **Private location** - Never criticize publicly
- **State facts** - "I observed X" not "You always Y"
- **Focus on behavior** - Not personality
- **Give chance to respond** - Hear their side
- **End with clear expectation** - "Going forward, I need..."

## üìã Communication Frameworks

### SBAR (Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation)
Use when reporting to your manager:
- **S**ituation: "We're behind on today's orders"
- **B**ackground: "Two people called out and we got extra orders"
- **A**ssessment: "We'll miss target by 20 orders"
- **R**ecommendation: "Can we get help from store or approve overtime?"

### GROW (Goal, Reality, Options, Way Forward)
Use for coaching conversations:
- **G**oal: "What are you trying to achieve?"
- **R**eality: "What's the current situation?"
- **O**ptions: "What could you try?"
- **W**ay Forward: "What will you do?"

### DESC (Describe, Express, Specify, Consequences)
Use for addressing problems:
- **D**escribe: "You've been 15 minutes late 3 times this week"
- **E**xpress: "This creates problems because..."
- **S**pecify: "I need you here on time at 8:00 AM"
- **C**onsequences: "If this continues, we'll need to..."

## üåç Multilingual Communication Tips
**For Non-English Speakers:**
- Speak slowly (not loudly)
- Use simple words
- Avoid idioms ("it's a piece of cake" ‚Üí "it's easy")
- Use pictures/demonstrations
- Write important information
- Use translation apps (Google Translate)
- Learn key phrases in their language
- Pair with bilingual buddy

**Body Language:**
- Thumbs up = Good
- Pointing = Direction
- Hand out = Stop/Wait
- Waving = Come here
- Head nod = Yes/Agree
- Shaking head = No
