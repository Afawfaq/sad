# Common Coaching Mistakes

What to avoid when coaching employees.

**Mistake 1: Waiting too long to address issue**
Fix: Address problems immediately when you see them

**Mistake 2: Being too nice/indirect**
Fix: Be clear and direct. "I need you to..." not "Maybe you could try..."

**Mistake 3: Not documenting verbal conversations**
Fix: Write notes after every coaching conversation

**Mistake 4: Treating everyone differently**
Fix: Apply same standards to everyone (consistency matters)

**Mistake 5: Making it personal**
Fix: Focus on behavior/performance, not person ("Your pick rate is low" not "You're lazy")

**Mistake 6: Having emotional conversations**
Fix: Cool off first, then address it professionally

**Mistake 7: Coaching in front of others**
Fix: Always pull them aside privately

## âœ… Coaching Success Indicators

**You know it's working when:**
- Performance improves within set timeline
- Employee takes ownership of issue
- You see effort to change behavior
- They come to you with problems/questions
- Team morale improves

**You know you need to escalate when:**
- No improvement after multiple conversations
- Employee argues or denies issues
- Problems get worse, not better
- Affecting team or customers
- You've reached final warning stage

## ðŸŽ“ Remember

**Your job as coach:**
- Set clear expectations
- Provide tools/training needed
- Give honest feedback
- Hold people accountable
- Remove obstacles

**Not your job:**
- Make excuses for them
- Be their friend
- Lower standards
- Fix their personal problems
- Keep giving chances forever

**Balance:**
- Be firm but fair
- Be direct but respectful
- Be consistent but flexible (legitimate issues)
- Document everything but don't make it personal
